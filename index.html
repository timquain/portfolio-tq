Artifact Information: JayTees Fabric Assistant (AI E‑commerce Chatbot)

Artifact Title

JayTees Fabric Assistant — AI‑Powered E‑Commerce Chatbot

Introduction

This artifact is a working prototype of an AI shopping assistant for a boutique fabric business. It helps customers discover fabrics, estimate yardage, read care instructions, and understand policies through natural conversation. The prototype was developed during an AI Lab exercise.

Artifact Description

Objective: Design and implement a helpful, safe, and testable AI assistant that improves customer decision‑making and reduces repetitive support.

Process: Empathize with users → Define the problem → Ideate intents and flows → Prototype (FastAPI + OpenAI) → Test (smoke, scenarios, unit tests) → Iterate (prompt tuning, cross‑sell rules, retrieval quality).

Was it a group or individually created product? Individually created; collaboration‑ready architecture with clear runbook and testing utilities.

Tools and Technologies Used: Python, FastAPI, Uvicorn, OpenAI API, YAML scenario tests, unittest, JSON product catalog, HTML/JS chat UI, runtime config and feedback logging.

Artifact‑Specific Value Proposition

Unique Value: Demonstrates end‑to‑end product thinking: user empathy, conversational design, safety guardrails, measurable testing, and deploy‑ready backend.

Relevance: Aligned to my personal value proposition by showcasing both UX clarity and technical execution; relevant to audiences evaluating practical AI skills for real‑world applications.

Customization for Audience

Adaptations Made: Branded as “JayTees Fabric Assistant,” added concise tone and clarifying questions, product cards, and optional visual UI. Provided a verification runbook and a portfolio page for academic or hiring review.

Relevance: Customizations ensure the artifact is accessible to non‑technical reviewers while retaining technical depth for developers.

Reflection

Significance: I chose this artifact because it represents the fusion of AI capability with human‑centered design. It demonstrates practical skill across prompts, back‑end services, and evaluation tooling.

Lessons Learned: I learned how to structure AI prompts around user intents, tune response behavior with runtime config, and validate outcomes with automated tests. I also reinforced the importance of guardrails, concise UX copy, and iterative refinement.

Feedback and Revisions

Feedback: Early feedback emphasized clarity of instructions and a desire for a visual demo. I incorporated a runbook, smoke tests, and a branded UI concept with persistent history.

Revisions: Improved onboarding (README and runbook), better error handling in test scripts, added /config and /feedback endpoints, and refined prompts for specific intents (yardage, care, policies).

References

FastAPI documentation; OpenAI API reference; internal runbook and testing scripts developed during the AI Lab.

Artifact Information:Deep Learning and Neural Networks

Artifact Title

Deep Learning and Neural Networks for Chemistry, Materials Science and AI Driven Discovery

Introduction

This artifact presents how deep learning and neural networks can strengthen research in chemistry and materials science. Drawing from my background in physical and computational chemistry and my MS training in artificial intelligence (data analytics specialty), this artifact shows how neural networks can accelerate molecular modeling, predict material properties, analyze experimental data, and enhance data driven scientific discovery for R & D and semiconductor related applications.

Artifact Description

Objective: To demonstrate how neural networks and deep learning can be applied within chemistry, materials science, and AI driven modeling to improve prediction accuracy, accelerate simulations, and uncover complex structure–property relationships that traditional computational methods struggle to capture.

Process: I consolidated my experience in computational chemistry, including DFT, adsorption modeling, MOF research, and single molecule junction studies, with advanced concepts from deep learning such as feedforward networks, convolutional architectures, and graph neural networks. I mapped real use cases to my field, including prediction of gas adsorption in porous materials, approximation of DFT level properties, time series analysis for STM based conductance data, and semiconductor relevant materials screening. I synthesized domain knowledge, AI principles, and insights from coursework into a structured, portfolio ready artifact.

Was it a group or individually created product? This artifact is an individually created product.

Tools and Technologies Used: Python, TensorFlow, PyTorch, Scikit learn, Jupyter Notebook, Materials Project data, DFT outputs from SIESTA and Quantum ESPRESSO, molecular descriptors, graph based modeling techniques, and data analytics concepts.

Artifact-Specific Value Proposition

Unique Value: This artifact demonstrates my interdisciplinary ability to merge physical chemistry, materials science, and artificial intelligence. It shows how deep learning can serve as a surrogate for expensive simulations, accelerate discovery pipelines, and enhance interpretation of complex experimental datasets. It communicates my capability to design AI models grounded in scientific principles.

Relevance: Industries such as semiconductors, energy storage, catalysis, and biopharma increasingly rely on AI enhanced materials modeling and data centered research. This artifact highlights how my skillset can contribute to modern R & D environments where scientific reasoning and machine learning are integrated.

Customization for Audience

Adaptations Made: I designed the artifact to be accessible to non specialists while maintaining technical rigor for scientific and engineering stakeholders. The language focuses on clarity, application, and measurable research impact.

Relevance: The examples chosen align directly with my background in MOFs, molecular separations, single molecule electronics, and DFT modeling, as well as my career interests in materials informatics, semiconductor analytics, and AI driven scientific computation.

Reflection

Significance: This artifact formalizes the link between deep learning and my established strengths in computational modeling. It demonstrates my evolution into an AI enabled scientist capable of developing hybrid, physics informed ML solutions for complex scientific problems.

Lessons Learned: Developing this artifact helped me articulate how neural networks complement physical models rather than replace them. I learned the importance of reliable datasets, validation strategies, transparency, and responsible AI when applying machine learning to scientific systems.

Feedback and Revisions

Feedback: Preliminary feedback emphasized expanding the scientific applications, clarifying real use cases, and strengthening the alignment between deep learning concepts and my computational chemistry background.

Revisions: I revised the artifact by adding domain specific examples (DFT approximation, adsorption prediction, STM trace analysis), improving clarity, and aligning the artifact more closely with interdisciplinary R & D expectations.
